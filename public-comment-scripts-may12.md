# Public Comment Scripts — NYOS Board Meeting
**Tuesday, May 12, 2026 · 2-minute public-comment slots**

Five distinct, ready-to-read scripts for parents who want to address the board during public comment, in the suggested reading order. Each script targets a different documented fact and ends with one specific ask the board can answer. The asks are not characterizations or accusations — they are questions about records.

Drafted conservatively for slower or nervous speakers — roughly 200–215 words each, which fits comfortably inside a 2-minute slot. Anyone reading at a normal pace will finish under 1:45.

If five different parents each take one script, the cumulative weight is much greater than one person delivering all five.

---

## Speaking tips

- **Identify yourself briefly.** Name + "NYOS parent" or "my child attends NYOS." No need to elaborate.
- **Read at a deliberate pace.** A page reads fast in your head; the room hears it slower than you think. Pauses are your friend.
- **Look up at the board between paragraphs.** Twice is enough.
- **Each script ends with a thank-you.** That's intentional — it leaves the room on a respectful beat.
- **Don't pad if you finish early.** Stopping at 1:45 is fine; talking past the buzzer is what undermines the message.
- **Print a copy in 14pt or larger.** You will be reading from a podium, possibly in low light, possibly nervous. Big type matters.

## Segment 1 — Dr. Seay's dual role at DSS

**Concern:** A documented concurrent advisory role at a firm that subsequently received NYOS contracts — and whether it was disclosed.
**Approximate length:** 207 words · ~2:00

> Good evening. My name is [name].
>
> I want to put one fact on the public record this evening, and ask the board one specific question about it.
>
> The fact: while Dr. Alan Seay served as NYOS's Interim Executive Director from February through June of 2024, his name and photograph were also listed publicly on the corporate website of Dynamic Support Solutions — DSS — as their "Special Advisor, School Leadership." That is not a rumor. It is on the firm's Company and Team page, alongside the firm's CEO. His DSS biography names NYOS as one of the schools where he served as interim leader.
>
> DSS later received contracts from NYOS — one ratified in December 2024, another approved in July 2025.
>
> So my question: during Dr. Seay's interim tenure at NYOS, was that concurrent advisory role at DSS disclosed to this board on the open-session record? In a written conflict-of-interest filing, in board minutes, in any document parents can see?
>
> I am not asking the board to characterize what was discussed in executive session. I am asking whether the disclosure exists in any record that is, or could become, public.
>
> Thank you for your time.

## Segment 2 — The $0 on the Form 990

**Concern:** Transparency on how Dr. Seay's interim role was compensated, and what the board approved on the federal tax return.
**Approximate length:** 213 words · ~2:00

> Good evening. My name is [name], and my child attends NYOS.
>
> I want to ask the board about a single line on a public document — the school's most recent IRS Form 990, filed in May 2025.
>
> That filing covers July 2023 through June 2024. It lists Dr. Alan Seay as Interim Executive Director, working forty hours a week. And in every compensation column on that form, his pay is reported as zero dollars.
>
> A person doesn't run a $20 million public school full-time for nothing. The zero on the 990 means he was paid through some other entity — Leadership4School, Dynamic Support Solutions, somewhere. But that intermediary doesn't appear on the form either, including in the section that lists contractors paid more than $100,000.
>
> The same 990 answers "no" to the question of whether the school had a business transaction with a current or former officer or key employee.
>
> These are documents this board is responsible for approving. So I'd like to ask: did the full board review the 2023 Form 990 before it was filed? And if so, how were those specific answers — the zero, and the "no" to Line 28a — discussed?
>
> Thank you for your time and your service.

## Segment 3 — The December 10, 2024 ratification

**Concern:** A board contract that was approved *after* DSS was already engaged and paid. Asks who authorized that pre-vote work.
**Approximate length:** 207 words · ~2:00

> Good evening. I'm [name], a NYOS parent.
>
> I'd like to ask the board about a single word in your own December 10, 2024 minutes.
>
> On that date, the board voted on item 5.b — to "ratify and approve expenditures and DSS audit support contract as to be amended."
>
> That word, *ratify*, is significant. Ratification means the board is approving a contract that was already signed and money that was already spent. It means Dynamic Support Solutions had been engaged, was already doing work, and had already been paid — before any open vote.
>
> I'm not asking who at NYOS made that initial decision. I'm asking this board: who, by your own records, authorized DSS to begin work and to be paid before the December vote? And what did the phrase "as to be amended" cover — what were the amendments the board ultimately approved?
>
> If DSS started work under administration approval alone, parents would benefit from knowing the dollar threshold above which administration is required to come to the board *before* signing — and whether the pre-vote DSS work was within that threshold.
>
> These are answerable questions. Thank you for your time.

## Segment 4 — The audit corrective action plan

**Concern:** The relationship between the conditional FY24 audit acceptance and the DSS engagement ratified three weeks later. Asks for the corrective plan itself.
**Approximate length:** 215 words · ~2:00

> Good evening. I'm [name], and I have a child enrolled at NYOS.
>
> I want to ask the board about the relationship between two of your votes from late 2024.
>
> On November 19th of that year, in a special meeting, this board accepted the FY 2024 audit conditionally — and I'm quoting from your own minutes — "subject to completion of the procurement testing, in light of the corrective action plan, and review by the board."
>
> Three weeks later, on December 10th, the board ratified a contract with Dynamic Support Solutions for "audit support" — for work that had already been done.
>
> So the question I'd ask, respectfully: was the corrective action plan referenced on November 19th distributed to all members of this board before the December 10th vote? And was the DSS audit-support engagement understood to *be* the corrective action — that is, is DSS the firm being paid to fix what your independent auditor flagged?
>
> If so, parents have a right to see that plan, to see the procurement-testing findings that triggered it, and to understand what DSS was retained to do. If not, the December engagement is even more in need of explanation.
>
> Thank you for your time, and for what you do for our school.

## Segment 5 — The October 24, 2025 board training

**Concern:** A documented post-tenure financial relationship between Dr. Seay and NYOS, sixteen months after his interim role ended.
**Approximate length:** 207 words · ~2:00

> Good evening. My name is [name].
>
> I want to ask the board about an event that took place on October 24, 2025 — about six and a half months ago.
>
> On that day, this board posted a formal "Notice of School Board Training" — a category of meeting separate from a regular board meeting. The notice listed a single item: "NYOS Board Training with Dr. Alan Seay," held from nine in the morning until five in the afternoon, at Education Service Center Region 13 on Springdale Road.
>
> That training took place sixteen months after Dr. Seay's interim tenure at this school had ended — and while he was still publicly listed as Special Advisor at DSS.
>
> So: was Dr. Seay compensated for that day of training? If so, by NYOS, paid through which entity — himself individually, Leadership4School, or DSS? Was the engagement governed by a written contract, an invoice, or did it sit under an existing vendor relationship?
>
> I am not asking whether the training was useful. I am asking whether the financial relationship between Dr. Seay and our school continued past his interim role, and through what entity any money flowed.
>
> Thank you for your time.

## Closing notes for organizers

- **Coordinate before the meeting.** If five parents are using these scripts, decide who reads which so there's no overlap and no skip.
- **Sequence matters.** The order above is intentional: *dual role* sets the foundation, *$0 on the 990* is the documentary anchor, *December ratification* is what the board itself voted on, *audit corrective plan* is the bridge between the 990 and the DSS work, and *October 2025 training* is the present-day relationship. Each builds on the one before.
- **If only one or two parents speak,** Segment 1 (dual role) and Segment 3 (December ratification) carry the most weight as standalone statements. Both are about facts the board has personal knowledge of.
- **Bring printed copies.** One for the speaker, one as a handout to leave at the records officer's desk. The handout creates a paper trail.
- **Sign in for public comment** before the meeting starts; some boards close the sign-in list at the gavel. Verify the NYOS-specific procedure with the records officer beforehand.
- **Take photos of the sign-in sheet** and the agenda copy you receive on entry. Quietly, without disrupting. They become part of the record if the minutes are later disputed.
