Every Records Request, in the Open

A complete, public list of the Texas Public Information Act requests being prepared and filed in connection with this investigation — what each one asks for, why it matters, and where it stands.

← Back to the full report

Texas charter schools are governmental bodies under the Texas Public Information Act (Texas Government Code Chapter 552, applied to charters by Texas Education Code § 12.1051). When a parent files a request, NYOS has 10 business days either to produce the records, certify a date by which production will occur, or seek an Attorney General ruling claiming an exception. The list below is the full slate of requests this investigation is preparing — designed to be narrowly scoped, individually answerable, and filed in priority order so the most consequential records come back first.

How these requests are scoped

Each request below is intentionally narrow. Filing a single sweeping request invites a 10‑day extension, an AG‑ruling claim, or a fee estimate that can take weeks to negotiate. Filing multiple specific requests — each tied to a particular document type, date range, and subject — produces faster, more complete responses and creates a clean public record of which records exist, which are being withheld, and on what basis.

Every request specifies electronic delivery (free), a $100 cost ceiling above which an itemized estimate is required under § 552.2615, a FERPA carve‑out so any incidental student information can be redacted without holding up the rest, and a partial‑production clause so undisputed records aren't held hostage to a single contested category.

Dr. Seay's interim engagement & payments to Leadership4School / DSS

The contract that governs his $0-on-the-990 tenure, and every payment that flowed to him or his affiliated entities.

Submitted

Records sought

A note on costs and timing

Each request specifies electronic delivery (typically free) and a $100 cost ceiling above which an itemized estimate is required by Texas Government Code § 552.2615. NYOS has 10 business days under § 552.221(d) either to produce records, certify a date by which production will occur, or seek an Attorney General ruling for any portion claimed exempt. This tracker will be updated each time a request advances a status.

If you have records too

This tracker is the formal channel — every request below is or will be filed under the Texas Public Information Act. But many records that resolve open questions are already held by parents, former staff, and community members who have copies of board agendas, screenshots of public web pages, photographs of bulletin‑board postings, or recollections of meetings. If you have anything you believe is relevant, you can contribute through the project GitHub repository. Contributions can be attributed or anonymous, at the contributor's preference; unverified contributions are clearly labeled as such until independently confirmed.
