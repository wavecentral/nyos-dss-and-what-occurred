# The Interim Pipeline

**An independent, community-driven investigation into undisclosed ties between NYOS Charter School's interim leadership and Dynamic Support Solutions (DSS)**

🔗 **Read the report:** [wavecentral.github.io/nyos-dss-and-what-occurred](https://wavecentral.github.io/nyos-dss-and-what-occurred/)

---

## Purpose

This report documents a potential conflict of interest at NYOS Charter School, a publicly funded Pre-K through 12th grade charter school in Austin, Texas serving approximately 1,800 students.

In February 2024, Dr. Alan Seay was installed as Interim Executive Director of NYOS. Public records show that Dr. Seay simultaneously holds the title of **Special Advisor, School Leadership** at Dynamic Support Solutions (DSS) — a Dallas-based firm that provides accounting, CFO, payroll, IT, and audit services to charter schools. DSS subsequently received contracts from NYOS, including at least one contract exceeding $50,000.

This report was produced by NYOS parents to:

1. Document verifiable facts from public records
2. Clearly separate confirmed information from unconfirmed reports
3. Identify the specific questions that remain unanswered
4. Track active Texas Public Information Act requests seeking the records needed to answer those questions
5. Provide the NYOS community with a factual foundation ahead of the May 12, 2026 board meeting

**This report is journalistic in nature. It is not advocacy material.** Where facts are established, they are cited. Where questions remain open, they are framed as questions — not accusations.

## Key Findings

### Confirmed

- Dr. Seay is listed as **Special Advisor, School Leadership** on the DSS corporate website — the same firm that subsequently received contracts from NYOS.
- The NYOS IRS Form 990 lists Dr. Seay as Interim Executive Director (thru 06/24), working 40 hours/week, with **$0 in reported compensation** from NYOS across all disclosure columns.
- The $0 figure means his compensation was routed through an intermediary entity, not paid directly by NYOS.
- NYOS's conflict-of-interest policy relies on **voluntary self-disclosure** by officers and board members.
- Dr. Seay's DSS biography explicitly names NYOS as one of the schools where he served as interim leader.

### Unconfirmed — Pending Verification

- The Seay-to-Rozas leadership sequence at both Legacy Traditional Schools (2022) and NYOS (2024)
- December 2024 board vote ratifying a DSS audit support contract
- July 2025 board vote on DSS contracts exceeding $50,000
- October 2025 board training session conducted by Dr. Seay
- 2026 DSS sponsorship of NYOS golf tournament fundraiser
